SAFETY FIRST! Do not attempt to fight a fire unless it is small and contained, and you have a safe escape route.

  • Activate Alarm: Pull the nearest fire alarm and/or shout "FIRE!" to alert others.
  • Evacuate Immediately: Leave the area immediately. Close doors behind you to slow the spread of fire.
  • Call Emergency Services: Once you are safe, call your local emergency number (e.g., 911, 999, 112).

âœ… Prevention: Handling Flammable Items Safely

The best strategy is to minimize risks by following proper storage and handling procedures.

  • Read Labels: Always read and follow instructions and warnings on chemical containers.
  • Proper Storage: Store flammable liquids in approved, sealed safety containers in a cool, well-ventilated area, away from heat sources.
  • Keep Away from Ignition Sources: Never use flammable materials near open flames, pilot lights, heaters, or electrical equipment that could spark.
  • Ventilation: Ensure adequate ventilation when using flammable materials to prevent the buildup of ignitable vapors.
  • Disposal: Dispose of flammable waste (like rags soaked in solvents) in approved, labeled metal containers with lids.

ðŸ›‘ If You Notice a Spill or Leak

Immediate action can prevent a dangerous situation from escalating into a fire.

  1. Stop the Source: If safe to do so, stop the leak or close the container.
  2. Ventilate: Increase ventilation immediately (open windows, turn on exhaust fans).
  3. Eliminate Ignition Sources: Turn off all possible sources of spark or heat nearby.
  4. Contain and Clean Up: Use an appropriate absorbent material (e.g., kitty litter, spill kit) to contain and clean up the spill.
  5. Report: Report the spill to a supervisor or building management.
